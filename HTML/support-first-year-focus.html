Support First-Year Focus

A unique opportunity afforded to students in WSU’s First-Year Focus (FYF) living-learning community is the “Dine with Faculty” program, made possible thanks to grants from the CougParents Fund.

Professors teaching courses aligned with FYF can choose to hold alternative office hours in the dining facility for their students’ residence hall. At the close of Dine with Faculty’s second year, both professors and students gave it a thumbs-up.

In fall and spring semesters of the 2015-16 academic year, 19 FYF faculty dined 157 times with students, who often came to the table in pairs or groups. That means professors had well over 300 individual student contacts and enjoyed plenty of out-of-class conversations. As explained by an English 101 professor, “I found it provided a space that students felt comfortable in to discuss classwork as well as their college experience…(more so than) my office. This allowed me to engage in dialogues with them on how to develop strategies for being academically successful.”

According to a survey, students who talked to their teachers over one of these meals reported:

  • Dining with their instructor had a positive impact on their course experience (90%)
  • Conversations during these meals helped them get answers to their questions (79%)
  • Their confidence about an assignment or exam increased (67%)
  • Feeling better acquainted with the instructor (67%)

“I would like to try to figure out ways to expand these lunchtime meetings. It was very nice to talk to these students outside of class, and we did discuss a number of ‘concerns’—from the nuts and bolts of how to find resources to their sense of isolation in Pullman.” — Richard S. Zack, Entomology 101 Professor
